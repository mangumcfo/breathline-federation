# Mangum Executive Doodle Style Guide v1.0
## Illustration Standards for All Mangum Executive Series Books

**Style:** Hand-drawn doodle — Diary of a Wimpy Kid meets executive business
**Date:** April 28, 2026
**Author:** Tiger (CC-Tiger) for Kenneth Mangum

---

## Style Name
**Mangum Executive Doodle Style**

## Core Style Rules (MANDATORY)

1. **Hand-drawn doodle look** — lines should look like they were drawn with a ballpoint pen in a notebook. Slightly wobbly, imperfect, sketchy. NOT polished vector art.
2. **Pure black-and-white** — no color, no shading, no gradients, no halftones
3. **Simple character design** — round heads, dot eyes, simple expressions. Think Jeff Kinney, not Marvel.
4. **Light humorous touch** — funny situations, relatable executive moments, never mean or crude
5. **High contrast, print-friendly** — works at any size in PDF, EPUB, and paperback
6. **Clean white background** — minimal or no background detail. Focus on characters and one key prop.
7. **Aspect ratio:** 16:9 landscape (1408x768px from Grok)
8. **No character name labels in final versions** — remove "KENN" and "BYTE" text from production illustrations

## Recurring Characters

### KENN (The Executive)
**Description for every prompt:**
> A male executive with a large round head (slightly oval), simple dot eyes, small curved or wavy mouth (expression varies), short neat hair parted on the left side, wearing a button-down collared shirt with the top button open (no tie), sleeves rolled to elbows. Slightly lanky build. Doodle style — simple lines, not detailed.

**Expression guide:**
- Happy/satisfied: small upward curve mouth, relaxed posture
- Stressed/overwhelmed: wavy squiggly mouth, hands on head
- Confident: slight smile, pointing or presenting
- Skeptical: one eyebrow line raised, arms crossed

### BYTE (The AI Agent)
**Description for every prompt:**
> A tiny cute robot companion about the size of a coffee mug. Simple boxy rectangular body, round head with two dot eyes and a single antenna on top, stick arms, simple legs. Always smaller than KENN — the human is always visually in charge. Doodle style — very simple, almost stick-figure level.

**Expression guide:**
- Helpful: thumbs up, holding a chart
- Confused: question mark above head
- Proud: chest puffed out slightly
- Sheepish: looking down, arms behind back

### Secondary Characters (as needed)
- **Board Member:** Round head, glasses, crossed arms, skeptical expression
- **Analyst:** Smaller figure, messy hair, surrounded by papers
- **CEO:** Slightly taller figure, pointing enthusiastically

## Master Prompt Template

Copy this template for EVERY illustration. Replace only the bracketed sections.

```
Hand-drawn doodle illustration in the exact style of Diary of a Wimpy 
Kid by Jeff Kinney. Black and white only. Simple pen-drawn lines, 
slightly wobbly, like drawn in a notebook with a ballpoint pen.

The main character KENN: a male executive with a large round head 
(slightly oval), simple dot eyes, [EXPRESSION — e.g., "small satisfied 
smile" or "wavy stressed mouth"], short neat hair parted on left side, 
button-down collared shirt with top button open, sleeves rolled to 
elbows, slightly lanky build.

He is [ACTION — e.g., "sitting at a desk looking at his laptop" or 
"standing at a whiteboard presenting"].

[SCENE DETAILS — e.g., "The desk is covered in messy stacks of paper 
and spreadsheets. His coffee is spilling."]

His tiny robot companion BYTE (coffee-mug sized, boxy body, round head, 
two dot eyes, antenna, stick arms) is [BYTE ACTION — e.g., "on the desk 
giving a thumbs up" or "holding up a sign that says HELP?"].

[ADDITIONAL CHARACTERS if needed — e.g., "Three seated figures at a 
conference table: one with glasses and crossed arms, one taking notes, 
one leaning forward."]

Clean white background. No shading. No gradients. No color. Pure black 
lines on white. Do not include character name labels. Funny and 
relatable. 16:9 landscape aspect ratio.
```

## Placement Rules

- **Frequency:** One illustration every ~20 pages (5-6 per book)
- **Position:** Near a section/chapter opening, after the heading
- **Relevance:** Must be directly tied to the content of that section
- **No captions required** — the image should be self-explanatory

## Quality Gate

Before including any illustration:
- [ ] Looks genuinely hand-drawn (wobbly lines, imperfect)?
- [ ] KENN recognizable (round head, parted hair, button-down)?
- [ ] BYTE recognizable (tiny boxy robot, antenna, dot eyes)?
- [ ] Pure black and white (no gray, no color)?
- [ ] Funny but professional?
- [ ] Relevant to the chapter?
- [ ] No character name labels in the image?
