# Prompt for Grok: Book Publishing Strategy

**Context for Grok:**

Kenneth (Kenn) Mangum is a CFO/COO executive with 20+ years of Fortune 500 experience (Ford, Hunter Douglas, Henkel/Sun Products). MBA from University of Michigan Ross School of Business. He has written 5 books that are currently referenced on his professional website (mangumcfo.com) and resume as "Published Author (5 books)" — but they are not yet formally published through any recognized platform. They exist as completed manuscripts (PDFs/docx).

He needs to legitimately publish these books online in a way that:
1. Makes them **genuinely purchasable** so "published author" on his resume is bulletproof to any recruiter or employer verification
2. Generates **some revenue** (even modest — this is more about credibility than income)
3. Is as **fast and low-friction as possible** — he wants to get this done, not spend months in publishing limbo
4. Gives him **real ISBNs, real listings** on Amazon/etc., and real "buy" links he can put on his website and LinkedIn

**The 5 books:**

1. **Strategic Finance For Growth** — Comprehensive roadmap for entrepreneurial CEOs through all business lifecycle stages (~1.6 MB PDF, appears to be 100+ pages)
2. **Harnessing Artificial Intelligence to Unlock New Possibilities for the US** — Analysis of AI's transformative impact on economy, education, and labor markets (~322 KB PDF)
3. **Blueprint for Brilliance: Designing Your Life for Strategic Growth** — Personal development framework with Personal Growth Flywheel concept (~84 pages, converted from docx)
4. **XRP: Decoding Strategy, Technology, and Utility for Real-World Value** — In-depth analysis of XRP's technology and cross-border payment applications (~300 KB PDF)
5. **Cryptocurrency Decoded: From Basics to Mastery** — Comprehensive guide to blockchain, Bitcoin, Ethereum, DeFi, and NFTs (~6.2 MB PDF, largest book)

**What I need from Grok:**

1. **Best platform(s):** Amazon KDP, IngramSpark, Lulu, Barnes & Noble Press, Apple Books, Google Play Books — which ones, and in what order of priority? Pros/cons for Kenneth's specific situation (credibility-first, revenue-second, speed-third).

2. **ISBN strategy:** Does he need his own ISBNs (Bowker) or can he use free KDP ASINs? What's the credibility difference? What's the cost/time tradeoff?

3. **Formatting requirements:** What format do each platform need (ePub, PDF, specific KDP format)? Can he upload his existing PDFs or does he need to convert? What are the minimum requirements for cover art?

4. **Step-by-step action plan:** Give Tiger (Claude Code, his AI agent on the Tiger node) the exact prompt and steps to:
   - Prepare each manuscript for upload (formatting, front matter, copyright page, etc.)
   - Generate or source cover art for each book
   - Create accounts on the recommended platforms
   - Upload and publish each book
   - Set pricing (what price points for credibility vs. revenue?)
   - Get the "buy" links to put on mangumcfo.com

5. **Timeline:** How fast can he go from "manuscripts on disk" to "live on Amazon with buy links"? What's the realistic timeline?

6. **Legal/tax considerations:** Any LLC/DBA needed? Tax implications of book royalties? Copyright registration — worth it?

7. **Prompt for Tiger:** At the end, write a complete, ready-to-paste prompt that Kenneth can give to Tiger to execute the technical preparation steps (formatting manuscripts, generating front matter, preparing files for upload, creating cover art concepts, etc.).

**Constraints:**
- Kenneth is in Lehi, UT
- He wants this done fast — days not months
- Quality matters but perfection is the enemy of done
- The primary goal is legitimate, verifiable "published author" status that holds up to recruiter scrutiny
- Secondary goal is actual revenue from sales
- He already has the completed content — this is purely a publishing/distribution question

Please provide your full analysis and the Tiger prompt.
