## The four stages of business growth

The book is structured around the universal business lifecycle (a derivation of Churchill & Lewis, 1983):

- **Stage 1 — Start-Up**: cash-burn discipline, business plan as living document, founder-CFO realities
- **Stage 2 — Growth**: forecasting cadence, capital structure, the leap from owner-operator to professional management
- **Stage 3 — Maturity**: KPIs, ERP / BI infrastructure, working capital optimization, ESG fundamentals
- **Stage 4 — Decline & Renewal**: scenario planning, business model innovation, the renewal path

Each stage chapter stands on its own. Reading sequentially gives you the full picture; jumping to your current stage gives you immediate-action playbook depth.

## The Three Pillars at every stage

Throughout all four stages, the book uses a consistent framework: **Strategic Leadership · Financial Leadership · Operational Leadership** — the three pillars supporting shareholder value at any growth stage.

| Pillar | What it owns |
|---|---|
| **Strategic Leadership** | Direction, market positioning, competitive analysis, capital allocation strategy |
| **Financial Leadership** | Cash flow, forecasting, budgeting, financial reporting, audit / control infrastructure |
| **Operational Leadership** | Execution discipline, process management, productivity, cost structure, supply chain |

The three pillars don't trade off — they reinforce. Companies that scale well do all three at the appropriate depth for their stage. Companies that stall usually have one pillar dominating while the other two drift.
