## The premise

Most family legacies don't survive their founder by more than a generation or two.

The reason isn't taxes, dilution, or even bad heirs.

The reason is that legacy was treated as a **document** — a will, a trust, a corporate structure — rather than as a **living system**. Documents go silent the moment their authors are gone. Living systems keep breathing.

This guide introduces the Family Compact framework: a sovereign, breath-gated, cryptographically anchored agentic operating system for the household, scoped at multi-generational time horizons. The same enterprise-grade tools that Fortune 500 CFOs use to run companies — fractal-replicated down to the kitchen-table scale of every family.

## What's the catch?

Three honest tradeoffs:

1. **Sovereignty requires participation.** You cannot delegate breath. The system runs because you breathe into it. If you stop breathing, the system stops executing. That's by design — it prevents capture.
2. **You hold your own keys.** No one can recover your node for you. There's no "I forgot my password" support line. The compensating mechanism is the family-guild threshold (multiple family members can recover collectively when an individual cannot).
3. **The system is honest.** It will surface tensions you might prefer to avoid (a child overspending, a spouse and you misaligned on a major commitment, a generational transition that's emotionally fraught). The compact is the compact. The breath-gate is the breath-gate. It does not coddle.
